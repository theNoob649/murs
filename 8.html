The Bountiful Update

Minecraft 1.8

  • Released: September 2, 2014
  • Development: 52 snapshots, 3 pre-releases

1.8 spent nearly a year in snapshots — 52 of them, the longest development cycle Minecraft had seen. It is also the version that never really ended. More than a decade after release, 1.8.9 is still the default on a large share of competitive PvP servers, kept alive by the combat system that 1.9 would replace.

The Ocean Monument

The Overworld's oceans had been empty since they were generated. 1.8 put something at the bottom of them.

  • Ocean monuments are large prismarine structures generated in deep ocean, the first structure that genuinely requires preparation to raid — you fight it underwater, in the dark, without air.
  • Guardians defend it with a charging laser attack and a thorns-like damage reflection, and they are difficult to approach on their terms.
  • Elder guardians, three per monument, inflict Mining Fatigue III on any player in range, which all but disables pickaxes. Killing all three is the intended way to make the structure minable.
  • The reward is sponge — still the only block that removes water — along with eight blocks of gold hidden behind dark prismarine.
  • Prismarine, prismarine bricks, dark prismarine, and sea lanterns entered the building palette as a full set of blue-green blocks with animated textures.

Stone Got Complicated

Granite, diorite, and andesite generate in ordinary stone as large blobs, each with a polished variant. Nothing about them is functional — they exist purely so that stone builds have texture options, and so that caves are not uniformly grey.

Their arrival is also the reason a generation of players complained about mining. Suddenly a third of what a pickaxe hit was a block most people did not want, and diorite in particular became a running joke.

Building and Map-Making Tools

  • Banners in 16 colors, layerable with dozens of patterns, giving players a real heraldry system.
  • Armor stands, which display gear and, with NBT editing, became the workhorse entity for map makers and later for decorative builds.
  • Slime blocks, which bounce entities — and, critically, stick to adjacent blocks when pushed by a piston. Flying machines and moving redstone contraptions all start here.
  • Barriers, invisible indestructible blocks for adventure maps.
  • Spectator mode, letting players fly through terrain and view the world through a mob's eyes.
  • World border, a configurable, animated boundary with damage and warning settings.
  • Customized world type, exposing dozens of generation sliders directly in the UI.
  • New mobs: rabbits, with their own drops and a rare killer variant, and endermites, which spawn from ender pearl throws.
  • Commands: /execute, /blockdata, /entitydata, and /stats turned command blocks into a usable scripting environment.

Why 1.8 outlived itself: the version froze at exactly the moment competitive PvP had settled. Combat was click-speed, there was no attack cooldown, and shields did not exist. When 1.9 changed all three, servers with established playerbases stayed put — and many are still there.

A year of snapshots, and the version some players never left.
