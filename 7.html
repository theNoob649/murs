The Update that Changed the World

Minecraft 1.7.2

  • Released: October 25, 2013
  • Development: 14 snapshots, 3 pre-releases

The name is not marketing. 1.7 rewrote the world generator, and the result is the most visible dividing line in Minecraft's history — you can walk up to it in any world created before it and see exactly where the old terrain stops.

The Biome Explosion

Before 1.7 the Overworld had roughly a dozen biomes, and most were subtle variations on forest and plains. 1.7 more than doubled the count and, more importantly, added variants — hills, plateaus, and rare mutated forms of existing biomes.

  • Mesa biomes, built from banded stained clay in reds and oranges, with variants exposing gold at unusually high altitudes.
  • Savanna, home to acacia trees and their distinctive orange planks.
  • Roofed forest, a dense dark-oak canopy that stays dark enough for hostile mobs to spawn under it in daylight.
  • Mega taiga, with 2×2 spruce trunks, podzol, and mossy cobblestone boulders.
  • Ice plains spikes, packed-ice towers standing out of flat snow.
  • Sunflower plains and flower forest, arriving with a wave of new flowers — sunflowers, peonies, lilacs, rose bushes, and tulips in four colors — which gave several dyes a renewable source for the first time.

Two new wood types shipped with the biomes: acacia and dark oak, each with a full set of planks, stairs, slabs, and fences. Dark oak came with an unusual catch — its saplings only grow when planted in a 2×2 square.

Why It Broke Old Worlds

Biomes are placed by a layered noise system, and 1.7 replaced that system wholesale. A world that had already generated chunks under the old rules kept them — but any newly explored chunk used the new generator, and the two do not agree about where terrain should sit.

The result is a hard seam: cliffs where a plain should continue, oceans cut off mid-shoreline, rivers ending in a wall. There was no fix, only workarounds — players abandoned old worlds, explored past the seam and ignored it, or built along it deliberately. Nearly every long-running world from that era carries the scar.

Everything Else

  • Fish became real items. Salmon, clownfish, and pufferfish joined raw fish, all catchable, with pufferfish feeding the new Water Breathing potion.
  • Stained glass in 16 colors, blocks and panes — a request the community had been making since wool became dyeable.
  • AMPLIFIED world type, stretching terrain across the full build height into overhangs and vertical extremes.
  • Red sand, packed ice, and podzol as new building blocks.
  • Commands: /summon, /setblock, and /tellraw gave map makers precise control over entities, blocks, and chat for the first time.
  • Networking rewritten on Netty, logging moved to Log4j 2, and the achievement list was expanded.
  • Multiple resource packs could be layered at once, with a larger size limit and servers able to send one on join.

The trade-off: 1.7 gave the Overworld its variety, but it also established that world generation changes are effectively permanent for existing saves. Every later terrain overhaul — 1.18 most of all — inherited the same problem.

Most updates add things to the world. This one replaced it.
