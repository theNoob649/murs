The Horse Update

Minecraft 1.6.1

  • Released: July 1, 2013
  • Development: 18 snapshots, 2 pre-releases

1.6 is remembered for horses, and fairly so — they were the headline. But the update quietly did two other things that mattered more in the long run: it replaced texture packs with a real resource pack system, and it threw out the old launcher entirely.

Horses, and Everything That Came With Them

Horses did not originate at Mojang. They came from Mo' Creatures, a long-running mod by DrZhark, who was brought in to help adapt them for vanilla. That heritage shows in how much depth they shipped with for a single mob family.

  • Taming by persistence: you mount an untamed horse, get bucked off, and repeat until hearts appear. No item required.
  • Randomized stats: every horse rolls its own health, movement speed, and jump strength, so two identical-looking horses can perform very differently.
  • Breeding: golden apples and golden carrots put horses into love mode, and foals inherit from both parents — making selective breeding for a fast, high-jumping horse a genuine long-term project.
  • Donkeys and mules: slower, but they accept chests, turning them into mobile storage. A horse bred with a donkey produces a sterile mule.
  • Skeleton and zombie horses: undead variants, not naturally obtainable in survival at the time.
  • Horse armor in iron, gold, and diamond, found in dungeon and temple chests rather than crafted.

Leads arrived alongside them, letting players drag most passive mobs on a rope and tie them to fences — the first practical way to relocate animals without a boat or a long corridor of wheat. Name tags, renamed at an anvil, made any mob permanent and stopped it despawning.

The Decorative Block Wave

  • Hardened clay, made by smelting clay blocks, plus stained clay in all 16 dye colors — muted, earthy tones that builders immediately preferred to the harsh saturation of wool.
  • Carpets, crafted from wool, giving a thin colored surface that mobs cannot spawn on.
  • Hay bales, a compact way to store wheat that also breaks fall damage.
  • Blocks of coal, dense furnace fuel and compact storage.

Resource Packs and the New Launcher

Texture packs became resource packs, and the scope widened well past textures: sounds, fonts, and language files all became replaceable. The sound system was restructured at the same time, so packs could swap individual audio events rather than the whole set.

The launcher was rebuilt from scratch. Version selection, multiple profiles, separate game directories, and proper account handling replaced a tool that had barely changed in years. It is the reason players could still boot into 1.5 after 1.6 shipped — and the direct ancestor of every launcher since.

Under the hood: 1.6 also added the Health Boost, Absorption, and Saturation status effects, and the /spreadplayers and /playsound commands — unglamorous additions that map makers and minigame servers built on for years.

An update named after a mob, that mostly mattered because of a launcher.
