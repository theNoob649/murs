The Wild Update

Minecraft 1.19

  • Released: June 7, 2022
  • Development: 10 snapshots, 5 pre-releases, 2 release candidates

1.19 is two updates wearing one name. One half is the Deep Dark, the most deliberately hostile content Mojang has ever written. The other half is mangrove swamps, frogs, and a mob that fetches your items. They shipped together and have almost nothing to do with each other.

The Deep Dark

The Deep Dark generates in the deepest layers opened up by 1.18, and it is built around one idea: noise is something you can spend.

  • Sculk sensors detect vibrations — footsteps, blocks placed, doors, projectiles landing — and emit a redstone signal whose strength depends on how far away the vibration was.
  • Sculk shriekers do the same thing, but instead of redstone they scream. Each shriek applies Darkness, which pulses your vision down to almost nothing. Activate one three times with no Warden nearby and one arrives.
  • Sculk catalysts spread the biome. When a mob dies near one, sculk grows outward over the surrounding stone, sometimes generating new sensors and shriekers as it goes.
  • Ancient cities are the structure at the center of it: sprawling deepslate ruins with redstone-trapped chests, a large portal-shaped frame that does nothing, and the only source of Swift Sneak, which lets you crouch at near-walking speed.

The Warden Is Not a Boss

The Warden has 500 health, hits hard enough to kill a player in full enchanted netherite in a couple of blows, and fires a ranged sonic boom that ignores armor entirely and travels through blocks. It drops one sculk catalyst. That is the entire reward.

This is intentional. The Warden is blind, navigates by vibration and by smell, and burrows away if it loses track of you. It is the first mob in Minecraft designed on the assumption that you will not fight it. The correct play is Swift Sneak, wool to muffle your footsteps, and a snowball thrown across the room to send it somewhere else.

In a game where the answer to every threat had gradually become "get better gear," 1.19 added a threat where better gear does not help.

The Wild Half

  • Mangrove swamps: mangrove trees grown from propagules, tangled roots you can walk through, and mud — which dries into packed mud and crafts into mud bricks.
  • Frogs and tadpoles: a frog's variant is set by the temperature of the biome its tadpole grows up in, not by its parents. Frogs eat small magma cubes and drop froglight, one of the few genuinely bright decorative light blocks.
  • The Allay: winner of the Minecraft Live 2021 mob vote. Hand it an item and it collects matching drops and brings them back, or delivers them to a note block. It quietly became a serious item-sorting tool.
  • Boats with chests, goat horns in eight variants, and the recovery compass, which points to where you last died.

What got cut: the original Wild Update reveal at Minecraft Live 2021 showed fireflies and a reworked birch forest with denser, taller trees. Neither shipped. Fireflies were dropped after it was raised that real fireflies are toxic to frogs, and the birch forest overhaul was cut with nothing in its place. 1.19 is a good update, but it is noticeably not the update that was pitched.

The Deep Dark is the part people remember, and it earned that. The rest of the wild is still waiting.
