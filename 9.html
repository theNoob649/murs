The Combat Update

Minecraft 1.9

  • Released: February 29, 2016
  • Development: 8 snapshots, 4 pre-releases

1.9 is the most argued-about update in Minecraft's history. It rewrote melee combat from the ground up, and nearly a decade later parts of the community still refuse to use it — a large share of PvP servers run 1.8 to this day.

The Attack Cooldown

Before 1.9, melee combat was click-speed. Whoever could physically click fastest won, which turned fights into a test of mouse hardware and finger stamina rather than decision-making.

1.9 added a cooldown to every weapon. Attacking before the meter refills does drastically reduced damage. Each weapon type got its own recovery speed — axes hit hard but recover slowly, swords sit in the middle, and the whole system rewards timing over frequency.

It also introduced sweeping attacks: a fully-charged sword swing damages every mob in a small arc, making swords genuinely useful against crowds for the first time.

Shields and the Off-Hand

  • Shields replaced the old sword-block mechanic. They absorb damage from the direction you're facing, deflect arrows entirely, and can be decorated with banner patterns.
  • Dual wielding: a second hand slot let players hold a torch, map, shield, or food alongside their main weapon.
  • Tipped arrows: arrows brewed with potions, bringing status effects to ranged combat. Spectral arrows made targets glow through walls.

The End Got a Reason to Exist

The bigger change, and the one nobody argues about, is what happened to the End. Before 1.9 it was a single fight and a credits roll. After 1.9 it became a dimension.

  • Redesigned dragon fight: iron bars now cage the healing crystals, and the dragon cycles through more varied attack phases instead of circling predictably.
  • Respawning the dragon: place four End Crystals around the exit portal and the fight resets. Previously a one-time event, now a repeatable resource.
  • End Cities and End Ships: sprawling purpur structures in the outer islands, guarded by Shulkers — mobs that shoot homing projectiles which lift you into the air.
  • Elytra: found in End Ships. Gliding wings that fundamentally changed traversal for every player who got them.
  • Chorus fruit for random short-range teleportation, and the dragon's breath, needed to brew lingering potions.

The legacy: The Elytra, added almost as a side note in the End City loot table, ended up being the most transformative item in Minecraft. Combined with firework rockets in 1.11, it turned the Overworld from a place you walk across into a place you fly over.

1.9 didn't just change combat. It took the game's dead-end dimension and made it the endgame.
