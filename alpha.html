Minecraft Alpha

a1.0.0 — a1.2.6

  • Period: June 30, 2010 — December 3, 2010
  • Key versions: a1.0.1, a1.0.11, a1.1.0, a1.2.0 (Halloween Update), a1.2.6

Alpha is the era in which Minecraft became a business. It started on June 30, 2010, cost €9.95, and included a promise that every future update would be free to anyone who bought in at that price — a promise Mojang kept through the full release and beyond. Word spread, YouTube coverage exploded through the autumn, and by the time Alpha ended in December the game had gone from a hobby project to Notch's full-time job and then to a company.

Redstone and Survival Multiplayer

Alpha v1.0.1, released July 2, 2010, added redstone wire, torches, levers, buttons, and pressure plates. It arrived as a fairly modest circuitry system and turned into the foundation of an entire sub-community — the players who build calculators, sorting systems, and working computers inside a block game.

Survival Multiplayer launched in August 2010. Classic had had multiplayer for over a year, but only for building. SMP meant mobs, mining, and death shared with other people, and it is the direct ancestor of every Minecraft server that has existed since.

The Halloween Update

Alpha v1.2.0, released October 31, 2010, is the largest single update of the pre-release years and one of the largest in the game's history.

  • The Nether. A second dimension, reached by building an obsidian frame and lighting it. It brought netherrack, soul sand, glowstone, and lava seas — and a travel ratio in which one block moved in the Nether equals eight in the Overworld, making it a fast-travel network as well as a hazard.
  • Biomes. Terrain generation gained temperature and humidity, so worlds stopped being uniform green and started having deserts, forests, and snowy regions with appropriate trees and grass color.
  • New mobs: ghasts and zombie pigmen in the Nether, plus fences for the Overworld.
  • Better lighting, smooth lighting on blocks, and a considerably moodier atmosphere overall.

The dimension shipped named "the Slip" internally and hell-themed maps had existed as a curiosity since Indev, but this was the first version where a second world was a real, reachable destination.

Everything Else

Between the big releases, Alpha filled in a lot of gaps: boats, fishing, cake, lockable chests, and the shift to a shared client-server inventory in a1.1.0 that made multiplayer inventories behave properly. Sneaking arrived. Beds did not — those waited for Beta 1.3.

Frequently misattributed: pistons, brewing, enchanting, and weather are all commonly credited to Alpha and none of them are. Weather came in Beta 1.5, pistons in Beta 1.7, and brewing and enchanting in the 1.9 pre-releases just before full release in November 2011.

Alpha ended on December 3, 2010, with a1.2.6. Seventeen days later the game moved to Beta and the price rose to €14.95 — still under half of what the finished game would cost.
