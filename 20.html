Trails & Tales

Minecraft 1.20

  • Released: June 7, 2023
  • Development: 6 snapshots, 7 pre-releases, 1 release candidate

1.20 was announced at Minecraft Live 2022 without a name. That was new. Every previous update had arrived with a theme attached from the start, usually built around whichever mob had just won the vote. Mojang showed the features first and only revealed "Trails & Tales" months later, on March 2, 2023.

Archaeology

Archaeology had been announced back in 2020 as part of Caves & Cliffs, then pulled. 1.20 is where it actually shipped, and the design that arrived is deliberately slow.

  • Suspicious sand and suspicious gravel look nearly identical to the normal blocks. Hitting them breaks them and destroys whatever was inside.
  • The brush is the only correct tool. You hold right-click, the block reveals its contents over several seconds, and the item pops out intact.
  • Pottery sherds are the main find. Four sherds combine into a decorated pot, with each face showing its own pattern — a container that is also a piece of authored decoration.
  • Trail ruins are the dedicated dig site: a mostly-buried structure with a high density of suspicious gravel, and the only source of several sherds and armor trim templates.

Desert wells, desert pyramids, ocean ruins, and cold ocean ruins also generate suspicious blocks, which retroactively gave several long-ignored structures a reason to visit.

Armor Trims

Trims are cosmetic and that is the whole point. A smithing template plus a material — iron, copper, gold, lapis, emerald, diamond, netherite, redstone, amethyst, or quartz — stamps a pattern onto a piece of armor in that material's color, with no effect on stats.

Templates come from structures, one pattern each, so a full set of trims requires visiting bastions, ocean monuments, ancient cities, woodland mansions, and the rest. Templates can be duplicated with diamonds and their base block, which means the rare part is finding the first one, not hoarding it.

It is the first system in the game where late-game armor stopped looking identical between players.

Camels, Sniffers, and Cherry Groves

  • Camels seat two players, dash forward on command, and stand tall enough that most hostile mobs cannot reach a rider — a genuinely different mount rather than a reskinned horse.
  • The Sniffer won the Minecraft Live 2022 mob vote. It is extinct; you hatch one from a sniffer egg found by archaeology. Adults sniff out ancient seeds — torchflowers and pitcher pods — that exist nowhere else in the game.
  • Cherry groves generate on mountain slopes with pink-leafed trees, falling petal particles, and a full cherry wood set.
  • Bamboo became a wood type, including the raft — a distinct boat model — and the mosaic block, which has no equivalent in other wood sets.
  • Hanging signs, the calibrated sculk sensor (which filters vibrations by frequency), and chiseled bookshelves that store books and output redstone based on which slot you touch.

What it's actually about: nearly everything in 1.20 is decorative or expressive. Nothing here makes you stronger. Trails & Tales was Mojang betting that a large part of the playerbase builds and decorates rather than fights — and the bet landed.

The last update before the version numbers stopped meaning what they used to.
