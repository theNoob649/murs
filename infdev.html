Minecraft Infdev

February 2010 — June 2010

  • Period: February 27, 2010 — June 30, 2010
  • Key versions: inf-20100227-1, inf-20100327, inf-20100630

Infdev exists because of one feature, and the name says which. The "Inf" is for infinite: on February 27, 2010, Notch replaced Indev's boxed-in maps with terrain that generates as you walk, forever. Everything else in this four-month era is the work of carrying Indev's content across to a world with no edges.

Infinite Terrain

Minecraft worlds stopped having a boundary you could reach and stand on. Instead the game generates chunks on demand as the player moves, deriving each one from the world seed, which means a world only ever occupies disk space where someone has actually been.

The practical scale is difficult to overstate. The generated surface of a Minecraft world is vastly larger than the surface of the Earth — not by a factor you'd walk across in a lifetime. Exploration went from being a thing you finished to a thing you did.

The generator itself churned during Infdev. Early builds produced strange, blocky landscapes dotted with pre-placed brick pyramids. On March 27, 2010, Notch swapped in the generator that would carry through into Alpha, producing recognizably Minecraft-shaped terrain for the first time.

The Far Lands

Infinite isn't quite infinite. At roughly 12.5 million blocks from the origin, the noise functions driving terrain generation overflow and the landscape collapses into the Far Lands — enormous walls of stone riddled with holes, stretching vertically from bedrock to build limit.

It's a bug, not a design, and it survived from Infdev all the way through Beta 1.7.3 before being patched out. Reaching it on foot takes weeks of real playtime, which is exactly why several players have done it and streamed the whole walk.

Filling The World

An endless world needs things in it. Infdev supplied them.

  • Dungeons with monster spawners and chests — the first structure worth searching for.
  • Minecarts and rails, giving long-distance travel a mechanical answer.
  • Ladders, signs, and doors, filling obvious gaps in the building set.
  • Buckets, making water and lava portable.
  • Wooden and cobblestone stairs, plus 3D clouds and reworked fluid behavior.
  • Golden apples, an early rare consumable.

Worth noting: redstone did not exist yet. Circuitry arrived later, in Alpha v1.0.1 in July 2010, and Infdev's contribution to automation was the minecart, not the wire.

Infdev was a technical era rather than a content one, and it made the decision that defines Minecraft more than any other: that the world does not end.
