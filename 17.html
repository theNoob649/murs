Caves & Cliffs Part I

Minecraft 1.17

  • Released: June 8, 2021
  • Development: 22 snapshots, 5 pre-releases

Caves & Cliffs was announced at Minecraft Live 2020 as a single update: new cave generation, rebuilt mountains, a taller world, and a pile of new blocks and mobs to fill it. It did not ship that way. On April 14, 2021, Mojang announced the update was being split in two, and 1.17 went out with the contents and not the terrain.

The Split, Honestly

The stated reasons were the amount and complexity of the content and, explicitly, team health. World generation was the hard part. Changing the world height means changing how chunks are stored, how they're generated, and how every existing save is upgraded — all while the new cave and mountain generators were still being tuned. Shipping content on schedule and terrain later was the honest tradeoff.

Postponed to 1.18:

  • The rewritten cave generation — noise caves, cheese and spaghetti caves, aquifers, lush caves and dripstone caves as actual biomes.
  • The rebuilt mountain generation and its sub-biomes.
  • The increased world height and depth, from Y -64 to 320.

The result is a version where you can find glow berries and dripstone as scattered decoration but not the biomes they were designed to fill, and where the mobs added for lush caves have no lush caves to live in. Mojang shipped an experimental data pack alongside the snapshots so players could preview the new generation, which was a reasonable stopgap and not a substitute.

Copper and the New Stone

Copper is the first ore added since the original set, and its trick is that it changes over time. Copper blocks oxidize through four stages — copper, exposed, weathered, oxidized — drifting from orange to green on their own. Apply honeycomb to wax a block and it freezes at that stage permanently, which gave builders a full gradient palette that they control. Its only functional use in 1.17 is the lightning rod, which redirects nearby lightning strikes and emits a redstone pulse when hit.

  • Deepslate replaces stone at low depths, with deepslate variants of every ore and a full set of building blocks including cobbled, polished, tiles, and bricks.
  • Raw ore items and raw ore blocks for iron, copper, and gold, separating what you mine from what you smelt.
  • Tuff and calcite, generating around geodes and in deep terrain.
  • Amethyst geodes: hollow spheres of tuff, calcite, and amethyst hidden underground. Budding amethyst grows clusters that regrow after harvest, making amethyst shards renewable and geodes worth protecting rather than stripping.
  • Tinted glass, which blocks light while staying transparent — a combination that had never existed.

Mobs and Items

  • Axolotls: five colour variants, tameable only by bucket, and they attack most aquatic hostiles. Killing a target while an axolotl helps grants Regeneration and clears mining fatigue — a direct counter to guardians.
  • Goats: mountain mobs that ram players and mobs off ledges and jump absurdly high. They drop goat horns.
  • Glow squid: the winner of the Minecraft Live 2020 mob vote. Drops glow ink sacs, which make sign text glow in the dark.
  • Spyglass, crafted from copper and an amethyst shard, for zooming in on distant terrain.
  • Candles in all 16 colours, placeable up to four per block, and the first genuinely small-scale light source.
  • Powder snow, which you sink into and freeze in, unless you wear leather boots. Collectible with a bucket, and a real hazard.
  • Glow lichen, cave vines with glow berries, moss blocks, and azaleas, the decoration set built for caves that had not arrived yet.
  • Sculk sensors, a wireless redstone component that detects vibrations. Their home biome, the deep dark, and the warden that lives there did not arrive until 1.19.

Judging it fairly: 1.17 is a strong content update wearing the name of a terrain update. Taken on its own it added copper, amethyst, deepslate, three mobs, and the first wireless redstone. Taken as the thing that was promised, it is visibly half a release — and Mojang said as much rather than pretending otherwise.

1.18 arrived five months later and delivered the other half. Together they are one of the largest changes the game has ever made. Apart, 1.17 is the strangest-shaped update in Minecraft's history.
