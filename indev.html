Minecraft Indev

December 2009 — February 2010

  • Period: December 23, 2009 — February 23, 2010
  • Key versions: in-20100110, in-20100128, in-20100223

Indev — short for "in development" — is the two months in which Minecraft acquired most of the systems people now think of as the game. Classic's Survival Test had proved players would accept danger. Indev built the economy that made danger meaningful: crafting, smelting, tools, food, and a day that turned into night.

Crafting and Smelting

The single most consequential addition of the era was the crafting grid. Blocks stopped being purely decorative and became inputs. Wood became planks became sticks became tools, and suddenly mining had a purpose beyond clearing space.

Furnaces followed, adding smelting: iron ore into iron ingots, sand into glass, raw food into cooked. The two systems together created Minecraft's first real progression ladder — wood, stone, iron, diamond — and that ladder has survived essentially unchanged ever since.

Survival For Real

  • Health became a persistent stat rather than a Survival Test experiment, with food eaten to restore it. (Hunger as a separate depleting bar wouldn't arrive until Beta 1.8, well over a year later.)
  • The day/night cycle arrived along with a visible sun and moon, giving the world a rhythm and making shelter a genuine need.
  • Dynamic lighting meant torches actually mattered. Darkness stopped being an aesthetic and started being a threat.
  • Tools and weapons in tiers, each with its own mining speed and durability.
  • Farming, letting players grow wheat rather than only scavenging.
  • Paintings, an early and slightly out-of-place concession to decoration.

Choosing Your World

Indev worlds were still finite, but for the first time you got to specify them. Before generating, players picked a theme (normal or hell), a shape (square, long, deep), and a size. A "hell" map came out red and gloomy — an aesthetic that would resurface properly when the Nether arrived in Alpha.

The maps were also enclosed, floating slabs surrounded by void or lava rather than the open oceans of Classic, which gave Indev a distinctly boxed-in feel that Infdev would demolish weeks later.

The forgotten mobs: Indev briefly hosted four test mobs built on MD3 models — Rana, Beast Boy, Black Steve, and Steve. They were removed before the era ended and never returned, but the name "Steve" stuck to the player character permanently.

Indev lasted two months and gave Minecraft crafting, smelting, tools, food, farming, and nightfall. Almost everything after it is refinement of decisions made in this window.
