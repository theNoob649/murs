The Nether Update

Minecraft 1.16

  • Released: June 23, 2020
  • Development: 19 snapshots, 8 pre-releases

For nine years the Nether was a corridor. You went in for blaze rods and quartz, built a nether-portal highway because eight blocks of Overworld per block travelled was too good to pass up, and left. It was one biome, one palette, and one mood. 1.16 gave it geography.

Four New Biomes

The Nether wastes still exist, but they are now one region among five, each with its own generation, hazards, and colour.

  • Crimson forest: dense red-stemmed huge fungi, weeping vines, and shroomlight. Hoglin territory.
  • Warped forest: teal, quiet, and the safest place in the dimension — no hostile mob spawns naturally there except endermen.
  • Soul sand valley: vast open caverns of soul sand and soul soil, littered with fossils and lit blue by soul fire. Ghast spawning is heavily concentrated here, and the sightlines are long.
  • Basalt deltas: jagged basalt pillars, blackstone, and constant ash particles. Magma cubes everywhere and terrible footing.

Crimson and warped fungi produce their own wood sets — stems, planks, doors, signs, the full family — giving builders two entirely new palettes plus blackstone and basalt variants.

Netherite

Ancient debris generates in small quantities deep in the Nether, most commonly around Y-level 15, and is blast-resistant enough that TNT and bed explosions became the standard way to strip-mine for it. Smelt it into netherite scrap, combine four scrap with four gold ingots for a netherite ingot, then use a smithing table to upgrade an existing diamond item.

The upgrade path matters. Netherite gear keeps the enchantments of the diamond tool it came from, so netherite isn't a fresh grind — it's the capstone on gear you already built. It offers higher durability and toughness, knockback resistance, and, most practically, it floats in lava and cannot burn. In a dimension made largely of lava, that is the feature people actually notice.

Piglins, Bartering, and Bastions

The Nether got an economy and a culture, both of which want your gold.

  • Piglins attack on sight unless you wear at least one piece of gold armor. Throw them a gold ingot and they inspect it, then drop something in return — ender pearls, obsidian, fire resistance potions, string, soul sand, and occasionally enchanted books. Mining gold in front of them makes them hostile regardless of what you're wearing.
  • Hoglins are aggressive food animals that flee from warped fungus and nether portals. Take one to the Overworld and it becomes a zoglin — permanently hostile, attacks anything, and never calms down.
  • Striders walk on lava and can be saddled and steered with a warped fungus on a stick, turning lava oceans into travel routes.
  • Bastion remnants are huge blackstone fortresses in four layouts, packed with piglins and the best loot in the dimension, including netherite scrap and the only naturally generated Pigstep music disc, composed by Lena Raine.

Quality of Life

  • Respawn anchors: set a spawn point in the Nether, charged with glowstone. Using one in the Overworld detonates it.
  • Lodestones: bind a compass to a fixed point that keeps working across dimensions.
  • Target blocks, which output a redstone signal scaled to how close a projectile hits the center.
  • Soul Speed, a boots enchantment available only from piglin bartering, that makes soul sand and soul soil faster than normal ground.
  • Chains, blackstone, gilded blackstone, crying obsidian, and twisting vines for building and climbing.

The shift: before 1.16 the Nether was a place you tolerated for ten minutes. After it, players build bases there. That is a hard change to engineer, and 1.16 managed it mostly by making the dimension worth navigating rather than by making it safer.

The Nether Update is widely held as one of the best releases the game has had, and the reason is straightforward: it took the game's most neglected space and gave it reasons to stay.
