The Exploration Update

Minecraft 1.11

  • Released: November 14, 2016
  • Development: 14 snapshots, 1 pre-release

1.11 had a problem to solve: the Overworld was enormous and there was almost no reason to leave your base. Its answer was to put something genuinely dangerous a long way from spawn, sell you a map to it, and make the reward worth the trip.

The Woodland Mansion

Mansions generate only in roofed forest, and only very rarely — they are frequently thousands of blocks from spawn, far enough that most players will never stumble onto one by accident. Inside is a maze of themed rooms, some purely decorative, some containing chests, and some containing mobs that were new to the game.

  • Vindicators carry iron axes and hit hard enough to end a poorly-equipped run in seconds.
  • Evokers do not attack directly. They summon fangs from the ground beneath you and conjure vexes — small flying mobs that pass through walls and ignore terrain entirely.
  • Together with the existing pillager-adjacent designs, these established the illagers as a faction: villagers gone wrong, with their own architecture and their own banner.
  • Evokers drop the Totem of Undying, which, held in either hand, cancels a killing blow and restores you with Regeneration and Absorption. It is the only item in the game that undoes death, and it made the mansion trip mathematically worth it.

Explorer maps, bought from cartographer villagers, point to the nearest mansion or ocean monument. They are the game's first structure-locating item and the mechanism that made both structures findable at all.

Shulker Boxes

The shulker box is the single most consequential item in 1.11, and it barely got mentioned in the announcement. Crafted from two shulker shells and a chest, it holds 27 slots — and when broken, it keeps its contents.

That one property changed inventory management permanently. A shulker box turns one inventory slot into 27, which means a single trip can carry an entire base's worth of material. Mining trips, moving bases, and large builds all stopped being logistics problems. They come in 16 colours, and unlike most dyed blocks, they can be re-dyed rather than recrafted.

The Rest

  • Observers, redstone components that emit a pulse when the block in front of them changes state. They made compact, tileable redstone circuits possible and are now in nearly every farm design.
  • Llamas, tameable in mountains and savannas, wearing carpets for decoration and chests for storage. Lead one and the rest of the herd follows, forming caravans.
  • Curse of Binding and Curse of Vanishing, the game's first intentionally negative enchantments — found only as loot, and impossible to remove at a grindstone-less anvil.
  • Shields buffed from blocking 66.6% of melee damage to blocking all of it, retroactively making 1.9's combat rework far more defensible.
  • Entity and block IDs were converted to lowercase-with-underscores, a breaking change for command blocks and map makers but the foundation of every modern data pack.
  • Fishing loot tables were reworked, and firework rockets could boost elytra flight as of the 1.11.1 follow-up — the change that turned gliding into genuine powered flight.

The pattern: 1.11's headline feature was a building you visit once. Its lasting features were a storage container and a redstone component. Updates are rarely remembered for what they were named after.

Sold as exploration, remembered for the box you bring home.
